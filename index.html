FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What is forced alignment?
Forced alignment is the process of automatically synchronizing pre-existing text (like a translated script) with audio timing. Unlike transcription, which creates new text from audio, forced alignment takes your existing text and determines exactly when each word or phrase is spoken.
Why can't I just use WhisperX or Montreal Forced Aligner?
Those tools work well for European languages, but consistently struggle with CJK (Chinese, Japanese, Korean) languages due to tonal complexity, character boundaries, and lack of whitespace word separation. Subtitle Sanctuary was built CJK-first with Qwen3, specifically addressing these failure modes.
Does my data leave my computer?
No. Subtitle Sanctuary runs 100% locally on your Windows machine. Your audio, video, and text files are never uploaded to any server. All AI processing happens on-device.
What are the system requirements?
Windows 10 or 11, 8GB RAM minimum (16GB recommended), approximately 500MB disk space. A dedicated GPU is not required but will improve processing speed if available.
What subtitle formats are supported?
Input: TXT, DOCX, PDF for text; MP4, MKV, AVI, MOV for video; MP3, WAV, FLAC for audio. Output: SRT, ASS, VTT subtitle formats.
What does "confidence scoring" mean?
Each aligned subtitle line receives a confidence rating (high, medium, or low) indicating how certain the alignment engine is about the timing. Low-confidence lines are flagged for manual review, so you can focus your QA time efficiently.
Can I process an entire season of episodes?
Yes. Pro and Team plans include batch processing — queue multiple files and let them process sequentially. Each file's language is detected automatically.
What's the difference between Free and Pro?
Free gives you 3 files per month (max 5 minutes each) with SRT export and a small watermark. Pro removes all limits: unlimited files, no time cap, all export formats (SRT/ASS/VTT), language swap, batch processing, and no watermark.
